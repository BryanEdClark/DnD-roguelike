Common rules and mechanics for quick lookup

ü§º Grappling (2024 PHB)

Action Required: Attack action - make an Unarmed Strike (replaces one attack if you have Extra Attack)
How it Works: Instead of dealing damage, target makes a Strength or Dexterity saving throw (target's choice)
Save DC: 8 + your Strength modifier + your Proficiency Bonus
Success (target saves): Grapple fails
Failure (target fails save): Target is Grappled (Speed = 0, can't benefit from speed bonuses)
Requirements: Target must be no more than one size larger than you. You need at least one free hand. You can only grapple one creature per hand.
Moving Grappled Target: Every foot you move costs 1 extra foot (2 feet total per foot moved) unless target is Tiny or 2+ sizes smaller
Escaping: Target can use their action to make a Strength or Dexterity saving throw against your Grapple DC (same DC as above)

üí• Shoving (2024 PHB)

Action Required: Attack action - make an Unarmed Strike (replaces one attack if you have Extra Attack)
How it Works: Instead of dealing damage, target makes a Strength or Dexterity saving throw (target's choice)
Save DC: 8 + your Strength modifier + your Proficiency Bonus
Success (target saves): Shove fails
Failure (target fails save): Push target 5 feet away from you OR knock them Prone (your choice)
Requirements: Target must be no more than one size larger than you

üé≠ Common Conditions (2024 PHB)

Stunned: Incapacitated, can't move, automatically fails STR/DEX saves, attacks against you have Advantage
Prone: Disadvantage on attacks, attacks from within 5ft have Advantage, ranged attacks from >5ft have Disadvantage, costs half movement to stand
Restrained: Speed = 0, Disadvantage on DEX saves, attacks against you have Advantage
Incapacitated (2024): Can't take actions or reactions, can't concentrate on spells, can't speak except for short utterances, roll Initiative with Disadvantage
Invisible (2024): Impossible to see without special senses, heavily obscured for hiding, attacks have Advantage, attacks against you have Disadvantage, roll Initiative with Advantage
Poisoned: Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks
Grappled: Speed = 0, can't benefit from speed bonuses, condition ends if grappler is Incapacitated or forced movement separates you

‚öîÔ∏è Initiative (2024 PHB)

How it Works: Roll 1d20 + Dexterity modifier
Surprised (2024): If surprised when combat starts, you have Disadvantage on your Initiative roll
Invisible (2024): If invisible, you have Advantage on your Initiative roll
Incapacitated (2024): If incapacitated when rolling Initiative, you have Disadvantage
Ties: DM determines order or tied creatures roll d20 (highest goes first)

‚ú® Spell Save DC & Attack Bonus

Spell Save DC: 8 + Proficiency Bonus + Spellcasting Ability Modifier
Spell Attack Bonus: Proficiency Bonus + Spellcasting Ability Modifier
Spellcasting Abilities:
  • Wizard, Artificer: Intelligence
  • Cleric, Druid, Ranger: Wisdom
  • Bard, Paladin, Sorcerer, Warlock: Charisma

‚ö° Concentration (2024 PHB)

Duration: Up to the spell's duration (ends early if you cast another concentration spell, have the Incapacitated condition, or die)
When Damaged: Make Constitution saving throw
DC (2024): 10 OR half the damage taken rounded down (whichever is higher), maximum DC of 30
Multiple Sources: Separate save for each source of damage
Note: About 170 of the nearly 400 spells in the 2024 PHB require Concentration

üé≤ Advantage & Disadvantage

Advantage: Roll 2d20, use the higher result
Disadvantage: Roll 2d20, use the lower result
Multiple Sources: Never stack - if you have Advantage and Disadvantage from any number of sources, they cancel out

üõ°Ô∏è Cover

Half Cover: +2 to AC and DEX saves (low wall, furniture, creatures)
Three-Quarters Cover: +5 to AC and DEX saves (portcullis, arrow slit)
Total Cover: Can't be targeted directly by attacks or spells

üò¥ Resting (2024 PHB)

Short Rest: At least 1 hour of downtime. Can spend Hit Dice to regain HP (roll Hit Die + CON modifier per die spent). Some features recharge on Short Rest.
Long Rest (2024): At least 8 hours (max 2 hours of light activity, rest must be sleep/light activity). Regain all HP and ALL spent Hit Dice (changed from half in 2014).
Interruptions (2024): You can resume a Long Rest immediately after an interruption, but the rest requires 1 additional hour per interruption to finish.
Starting a Long Rest (2024): You must have at least 1 HP to initiate a Long Rest.

üìú Game Rules

üèÉ Movement & Actions (2024 PHB)

Movement: You can move up to your speed on your turn
Action: One action per turn (Attack, Cast a Spell, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, Help, Hide, Influence, Magic, Ready, Search, Study, Utilize)
Bonus Action: Some spells, features, or items grant bonus actions
Reaction: One per round (Opportunity Attack, Ready action trigger, some spells)
Free Object Interaction: Once per turn (draw/stow weapon, open door, etc.)
Potions (2024): Drinking or administering a potion now takes a Bonus Action (changed from Action)
Weapon Draw/Stow (2024): You can draw or stow one weapon as part of an Attack action. This weapon doesn't need to be the one you're attacking with.
Unarmed Strikes (2024): Now have 3 options: deal damage, grapple, or shove. Characters with Extra Attack can mix these options (e.g., attack, then grapple).
Spells Per Turn (2024): You can only use one spell with a spell slot per turn, but you can also use cantrips, magic items, and scrolls with leveled spells on the same turn.

üé≤ Ability Checks

Formula: 1d20 + Ability Modifier + Proficiency Bonus (if proficient)
Skill Checks: Ability check using a specific skill
Difficulty Classes (DC):
  • Very Easy: DC 5
  • Easy: DC 10
  • Medium: DC 15
  • Hard: DC 20
  • Very Hard: DC 25
  • Nearly Impossible: DC 30

üõ°Ô∏è Saving Throws

Formula: 1d20 + Ability Modifier + Proficiency Bonus (if proficient)
Common Saves:
  • STR: Physical restraints, being pushed
  • DEX: Dodging area effects (Fireball, breath weapons)
  • CON: Poison, disease, concentration
  • INT: Mind-affecting illusions
  • WIS: Charm, fear, mind control
  • CHA: Banishment, possession

‚öîÔ∏è Common Combat Actions

Attack: Make one or more attacks (based on features)
Dash: Gain extra movement equal to your speed
Disengage: Your movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks
Dodge: Attacks against you have Disadvantage, you have Advantage on DEX saves (until start of next turn)
Help: Give ally Advantage on next ability check OR attack roll against creature within 5ft of you
Hide: Make Stealth check to become hidden
Ready: Prepare an action to trigger on a specific condition

üíÄ Death Saving Throws

When: Start of your turn while at 0 HP
Roll: 1d20 (no modifiers)
Success: 10 or higher (3 successes = stabilized)
Failure: 9 or lower (3 failures = dead)
Natural 1: Counts as 2 failures
Natural 20: Regain 1 HP immediately
Taking Damage: One failure (or 2 failures if critical hit). If damage >= max HP, instant death
